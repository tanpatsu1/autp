# Growth / Marketing / Monetization Perspective

## Role Scope

Growth should evaluate audience pull, shareability, positioning, pricing potential, and launch risk. Draft ideas locally only. Do not post externally, buy domains, run ads, charge money, or choose final positioning here.

## Questions To Answer

- Which direction would people immediately understand and want to try?
- What use cases could spread through screenshots, templates, recommendations, or word of mouth?
- Which audience has budget or willingness to pay later?
- What claims would be unsafe or unsupported?
- What onboarding or FAQ angle would make the product easier to adopt?

## Initial Market Recommendation

The easiest first audience is people who already collect purchase candidates across many tabs: fashion items, gadgets, furniture, cosmetics, gifts, travel options, and hobby gear. This group has a frequent pain, screenshots well, and does not require medical-grade trust or enterprise sales.

The first public-facing explanation should be concrete:

- "Save the links you are considering, add notes, compare later."
- "A private board for things you might buy."
- "Stop losing product links in chats, tabs, and notes."

The broader product can still be an AI-assisted bookmark manager underneath, but the first growth wedge should not lead with generic bookmark language. "Bookmarks" feels old and crowded; "decision board for saved links" is more emotionally useful.

## Monetization Hypotheses

- Free user value: Save a useful number of private links, add title/category/tags/memo/favorite, search, edit, delete, and switch card/list views. Free users should feel the core "I can find and decide later" loop without pressure.
- Possible paid upgrade: Higher saved-item limits, persistent cloud sync, advanced comparison fields, multiple boards, AI summaries, AI auto-tagging, price-watch style notes or alerts after review, export, shared boards, and premium templates.
- Buyer type: Individual shoppers and hobbyists first; later prosumers such as stylists, creators, students with heavy research needs, small teams, and people managing repeated purchase/research decisions.
- What must be true before charging: Supabase/Auth/persistence must be verified, privacy expectations must be clear, data export or account recovery should be credible, AI output quality must be bounded, and the free product must already solve a complete save-and-find loop.
- HumanGate items to avoid: No billing, paid plan changes, affiliate activation, ad spend, domain purchase, external posting, public launch, or claims of live paid functionality without explicit human approval.

## Paid Feature Candidates

- AI comparison brief across selected saved links.
- Automatic grouping and tag suggestions.
- Multiple purpose-built boards, such as "new laptop", "spring wardrobe", "trip options", or "research sources".
- Custom comparison columns, such as price, size, shipping, deadline, priority, or pros/cons.
- Saved decision history: why the user chose or rejected an option.
- Export to CSV/Markdown/PDF for research, school, or purchase approval.
- Shared decision boards for family, partner, classmates, or small teams.
- Reminder and revisit prompts for links marked as important.

## Positioning And Messaging

Best near-term positioning:

> autp is a private decision board for links you might need later.

Supporting copy options:

- "Save product links, notes, and favorites in one place before you decide."
- "Turn scattered tabs into a shortlist."
- "Keep the link, the reason, and the next decision together."
- "For shopping, research, and anything you are comparing."

Avoid these claims until later:

- "Automatically finds the best option."
- "Never lose any link again."
- "AI understands all your bookmarks."
- "Tracks every price automatically."
- "Recommends the best hospital, doctor, or medical service."

## Growth Recommendation Draft

- Best acquisition angle: Lead with shopping and purchase-candidate boards because the value is visible in one screenshot and understandable in one sentence. Use fashion, gadgets, furniture, gifts, and travel planning as examples.
- Best retention loop: Saved links become more valuable when the user returns to compare, update notes, favorite finalists, and mark decisions. Later, reminders and AI summaries can deepen the loop, but the first loop is "save now, decide later".
- Best monetization candidate: Paid organization and AI assistance for people with many recurring decisions: multiple boards, custom comparison fields, AI summaries, exports, and sharing.
- Biggest launch risk: If autp is marketed as a generic bookmark manager, it may sound interchangeable with browser bookmarks, Notion, Raindrop, Pinterest, or note apps. If marketed too narrowly as fashion or hospital research, it may lose broad adoption or create trust risk.
- Questions for Product / Design / Review Gate: Should the next direction be framed as "shopping decision board" or "personal decision board" in-product? Which comparison fields can be introduced without expanding MVP too far? What privacy copy is required before examples include sensitive use cases? Which claims should be blocked until Supabase/Auth/persistence and AI behavior are verified?

## Risks And Tradeoffs

- Competition risk: Browser bookmarks, Notion, Pinterest, Raindrop, spreadsheets, and note apps already save links. autp needs a sharper promise: private saved links with decision context, not just another place to store URLs.
- Scope risk: Shopping boards can tempt price tracking, affiliate links, and automatic scraping. Those should be later reviewed features, not assumed in the next step.
- Trust risk: Medical, finance, and high-stakes comparisons should stay private-user-note use cases until legal, privacy, and claim boundaries are reviewed.
- Monetization risk: Charging too early can weaken adoption. The free plan should prove the habit before asking for payment.
- AI risk: AI can be the differentiator, but unsupported AI claims can damage trust. Position AI as assistance for organization and summarization, not authority.

## Decision-Ready Recommendation

Growth recommends using "shopping / purchase decision board" as the first expansion wedge, with "AI-assisted bookmark manager" as the underlying long-term platform direction. Fashion/brand management should be a strong template and visual sub-use-case. Student research can be a secondary acquisition use case. Hospital/service comparison should be treated cautiously as a private organization pattern, not a headline growth direction.

This recommendation does not request external posting, billing, paid plans, affiliate activation, production launch, or new implementation in this pass.
